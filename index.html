Chapter Four and Five: Reflections

There are many ways of producing websites weather it be a web developer, a website to help you create a website, teamwork, and more. There is a common format for websites starting with a home page. As helpful as this is, modern web designers have prioritized efficiency and control. Solving problems with websites regarding templates brings familiarity. In today's age, programs and templates have modified. Websites are designed linearly, telling the story from the start to the end. This secretly manipulates the reader's mood. The author argues that compression (organizing everything into a neat, measurable structure) takes away freedom and unpredictability. Websites consider designing around the answers and standardizing user behavior while making it interactive. Users truly want control over how they are viewing their content. The article explains that Scott's clients trust him for his simplicity and realism. He explains how autonomy is important to him. Overall, a lot of websites lack curiosity witch is a key factor for a web designer's goal, while attempting to keep the user in the “box of control” they make their website to be. The author explains that aricechur and web development are similar. The author argues that both create spaces for interaction and feeling. Being a web developer feels more flexible to them. A simple website can be argued as a myth. Beneath its beauty lie tools to construct this website, which are anything but simple. Web developers are expected to keep up with technology and always find a way to tighten their design. They want to keep users from feeling lost or having questions. Overall, the author chose web development over architecture because of its vast choice in flexibility. Even though neither is “simple,” at the end of the day, it may keep users from thinking deeper and having meaningful engagement.
